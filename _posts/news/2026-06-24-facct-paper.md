Bonjour! I'm in Montreal presenting a new paper with Diag Davenport at *ACM FAccT:* ["Putting the 'Practice' in Critical Technical Practice: A Decision-Making Pedagogy for the Public Interest Technology Clinic"](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3805689.3806542). 
<!--(I've also put together a friendly [one-page infographic](pdfs/Chambers_CSCW_2025_1p.pdf) about it.) -->
<!--more-->
This work describes our pedagogical approach in designing and running the graduate [Public Interest Technology Clinic](https://www.ischool.berkeley.edu/programs/clinics/public-interest-tech-clinic) at Berkeley, and motivates why similar experiential educational models are effective at training future critical technical practitioners.
